-- MY THOUGHTS --
Micro-Novella: under 50 pages. I will admit, I was hesitant to agree to read this for the author for several reasons, based on previous experience and NOT person preference. Right away, I thought this novella may have a few strikes against because it's been my experience that 1) novellas of this length rarely create a full, dynamic story. 2) Romance stories written by male authors have been more graphic and crass than romantic and/or erotic. And 3) I've really had a hard time connecting with many British authors. It's not the language and terminology, because I generally enjoy discovering a "footpath" in place of sidewalk, "crisps" in place of chips or "windscreen" in place of windshield.
Despite all of that, I vowed to myself when I began writing reviews more seriously that I'd be 100% honest and fair with every book I read. I agreed to read the book, because I was asked to. Simple as that. So, these are my findings on this novella:
It is written in a third-person format, but obviously from the male's POV. That is something we do not often have the opportunity to experience. The synopsis was off-putting because I have zero-tolerance for cheaters, but I'm well aware that they are out there and felt that regardless of my personal feelings, I'd still give it a fair and honest assessment. That it is the babysitter that is the temptress is rather cliche though and woefully unoriginal. On the bright side, the characters are written well. The dialogue and interaction feel believable and the flow of the writing is really good. There are a few parts that are so distinctly "foreign" that I felt like I needed a translator app to fully comprehend it, but that did not detract from the quality or experience. While the first half of the novella has a bit of plot working for it, the full second half is a detailed, play-by-play sex scene. There's no resolution other than the fact that they both got off and the conclusion is "to be continued". It is no more a stand-alone novella than the pilot episode of a new TV program would be. If "cheating spouses" is a sub-genre of romance that you enjoy, I'd probably wait for the rest of the novellas to release and read it as a whole. While I'm not a fan of the sub-genre and topic, I was very pleased with the quality of writing and flow in this novella.

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

Sweet Temptations: The Babysitter by Dark Inferno is a well-written erotica eBook. It’s very short – just 35 pages, and it is a fast read – I finished it in one sitting; I think it took about a half hour. I was pulled in pretty quick by the main character, Richard and what’s going on in his life. I knew that obviously this story would have a focus on the babysitter, so I was waiting for it to get to that point and I was interested to see how it would come about.

The story starts at a work party that Richard’s attended with his wife. At first his wife sounds a little controlling (would flip if he drank), but then she comes over and catches Richard’s hot boss kind of hitting on him and the wife just makes out with him like crazy. While this is happening I’m thinking, man, this guy is wanted by different women and he has a wife who clearly still gets him hot and bothered…so why will another temptation even come to light? His life is great as it is, why complicate it?

Then they come home from the work party and the babysitter is there and she is sweet and twenty-something, but Richard isn’t really giving her too much attention. She asks if he can fix her computer while her dad is out of town, and she lives in the apartment above them (or below, can’t remember). Richard doesn’t think much of it, but his wife does and she places the idea in his head that the babysitter is into him. And get this – the wife is ready to sleep with him right then after the babysitter leaves. And he says NO even though this is all he wants all night long. AGH. This makes me frustrated.

And then you guessed it- hello-oo, babysitter.

So, this book is strictly just a pure sexual fantasy. That’s all it is. I tried to let go of reality a bit while reading to remember this is fiction, this is not real. But it was hard because the author created a character that seemed decent and loves his wife, and then BAM cheats on her. The cheating factor just rubbed me the wrong way and stayed under my skin the entire story, so as much as I tried I couldn’t stop thinking about that. The wife wanted to have some, so why look elsewhere?! Also, the wife is clearly not dumb; doesn’t she know what’s happening? Couldn't she hear them through the walls? I’m wondering what comes next when the wife finds out. How does this story really end?

I think this is written for men as pure hot fantasy. I saw some other reviews that had the same thoughts as me – all written by women and all bothered by the cheating. I kind of hate to see things like this in print because I don’t like anyone, even in fantasy, making it seem like it’s OK to cheat. Not even OK, but that the desire is even there. Especially if you already have a great love life with the person you chose to have that love life with. Why look elsewhere? Okay, I realize that this story was not meant to anger me or make me ask these questions, but I can’t help it. That’s the reaction I had.

If you are looking for erotica that is well written (because it really is despite my frustrations) and you just want a quick escape then I definitely recommend this.
